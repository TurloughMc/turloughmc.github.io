System Communication

"The number one principle in Jakob Nielsen’s 10 Heuristics of usability is ‘The visibility of System Status’" - NNGroup

Questions we are trying to answer;

  1. What are the Factors to consider with communication of system status?
  2. What are the Communication Methods?
  3. What are the Components to support the communications?

Key Factors

The best way to communicate system status varies depending on several key factors:

  • The type of information being communicated
  • The urgency of the information — how important it is that the user sees it immediately
  • Whether the user needs to take action as a result of the information

This can be Abstracted to;

  • Message scope from/to; Global v Contextual
  • Triggered by; System Event v User Action
  • Whether message should be; Disruptive v Non Disruptive
  • Information prompt; Passive v Active

What are the Communication Methods?

  • Indicators provide supplementary information about a dynamic piece of content or UI element. They are conditional - that is, they may appear or change under specific conditions.
  • Validations are tied to a user’s action or input, communicating whether the data just entered was incomplete or incorrect (or possibly correct).
  • Confirmation - are you sure Dialog?
  • Acknowledgements - active and passive
  • Empty State - content has failed to load or sync

Material Design

Break the Notifications (not Indicators or Validation) types of message into 2 types;

  • Confirmation - Contextual, Active, Intrusive. Delivered by a Component it calls 'Alert Dialog' (ie. much like Action-required notifications)
  • Acknowledgement - which can be delivered by a variety of Components depending on Urgency level, Inclusion of an action to correct a problem and Duration on screen (transient, dismissable, or both)

In Summary for Toolkit

  • Errors on a Detail view should give a summary of Error issues at the top or above the Save button, as well as inline with the individual fields.
  • Deletions should use a Toast or a Modal > Toast depending on whether the delete is significant or not
