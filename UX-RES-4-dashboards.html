What is a data dashboard?

A data dashboard is an information management tool that visually tracks, analyzes and displays key performance indicators (KPI), metrics and key data points to monitor the health of a business, department or specific process, to help make data-driven decisions.

Dashboard definition

  • Visual display of
  • the most important information needed to achieve one or more objectives which
  • fits entirely on a single computer screen so it can be
  • monitored at a glance

Dashboard types by Business activity

This classification is the only one that significantly relates to differences in visual design

  • Operational - often used across various levels of an organization, to understand in real-time, if its performance is on target
  • Analytic - help organizations establish targets based on insights into historical data with ownership usually falling on business analysts/experts
  • Strategic - typically supporting managers, focusing on high-level measures of performance, including forecasts to light the path into the future

Operational Dashboards

Look at current routine functioning and activities of an organization related to their KPIs. n our case the operation of Patient Care (for example - Clinical) or Data Collection (for example in a Registry)

  • Dynamic and immediate
  • Display must be very simple

Analytical dashboards

Analyse - examine (something) methodically and in detail, typically in order to explain and interpret it.

This could be done after the Data has been collected to see what the cause and affects can be ascertained about the disease

This could also be used to analyse reasons for the Data being the way it is Eg. Centre X Users weren't trained to entry the Data correctly

Analytic

  • Greater context - rich comparisions , more extensive history, and subtler performance evaluators
  • Static snapshots - no constant changing
  • More sophisticated display media more useful for analysts
  • Support interactions with data - drill downs etc. to examine causes

Strategic Dashboards

  • Extremely simple display mechanisms
  • Long-term strategic direction - Benefit from static snapshots taken over monthly, weekly or daily
  • Unidirectional displays - without interaction that would be needed to support further analysis

Strategic dashboards

Strategic - Relating to the identification of long-term or overall aims and interests and the means of achieving them.

Typically created for Management, CMO and CFO

What type of Analytics are we doing?

Descriptive, predictive, and prescriptive analytics

Depending on the goals of your data initiative, and how you’re measuring patient experience (or other task) KPIs, you will be focusing on either descriptive, predictive, or prescriptive analytics—and perhaps all three.

Descriptive analytics

Is the interpretation of historical data/record to better understand what has already occurred.

This is based on the majority of all healthcare data, and includes claims data, clinical documentation, patient surveys, and lab tests. It’s report-based, and offers no trend of forecast analysis.

Predictive analytics

Uses descriptive data to forecast future events.

Predictive analytics is the use of data, statistical algorithms and machine learning techniques to identify the likelihood of future outcomes based on historical data. The goal is to go beyond knowing what has happened to providing a best assessment of what will happen in the future.

To conduct predictive analysis, an organization needs true interoperability, and more data than what’s found in electronic medical records (EMRs).

Prescriptive analytics

Prescriptive analytics makes use of machine learning to help an organisation/business decide a course of action based on a computer program's predictions.

Relies heavily on machine learning algorithms to indicate what course of action will yield the most significant benefit when a predicted event occurs. Prescriptive analytics in healthcare has a number of barriers to adoption: lack of skilled employees, regulatory issues, and too much data.
